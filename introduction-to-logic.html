Introduction to logic

Title: Introduction to Logic
Course code: 51221PLC3Y
Credits: 3 EC
Course load: 4 weeks programme (part time) / 2 weeks programme (fulltime)
Lecturer: Peter van Ormondt

This is an introductory course of logic and is part of the Minor Logic and Computation. This course is only offered as a self-study course and meets the entry requirement of the minor.

Course description

This course is intended primarily for students who wish to do the Minor Logic and Computation but who have not yet followed a suitable introductory course to logic. Students are expected to complete the course by self-study alone. The written exam will assess studentsâ€™ knowledge of the following topics

  • Propositional logic
    • Syntax, semantics
    • Functional completeness
    • Notion of validity
    • Truth Tables
    • Natural deduction
  • Predicate logic
    • Syntax, semantics
    • Interpretation by Substitution
    • Notion of validity
    • Identity
    • Properties of Relations
    • Natural deduction (minus identity)

Literature

You are free to choose your own textbook. There are many excellent introductions to logic available. However, for this course we recommend everybody to read

  • The first four chapters to the textbook of the course Logic in Action.

In addition, read,

  • Chapters 1-4 of Logic, Language and Meaning. Introduction to Logic, Volume 1, by L.T.F. Gamut. University of Chicago Press.

Or

  • Chapters 0-2 of Logic and Structure, by Dirk van Dalen. Springer

The book by Gamut is written with an eye towards applications in linguistics, whereas the book by Van Dalen has a more technical style which may seem more familiar for students with a background in mathematics or computer science.
